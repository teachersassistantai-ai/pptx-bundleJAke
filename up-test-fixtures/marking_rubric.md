# Marking Rubric — Mixtures & Separation Culminating Task (Year 7 Science)

| Criterion | A | B | C | D | E |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Particle-model reasoning** | Uses the particle model precisely to explain the physical properties of substances and to justify every separation stage | Uses the particle model to explain properties and justify most stages | Uses the particle model to explain the properties of substances and justify a separation process | Describes separation stages with limited particle-model reasoning | States a separation method with no scientific reasoning |
| **Designing separation processes** | Designs an efficient, fully justified multi-stage process that separates all components of the mixture | Designs a process that separates most components with sound justification | Develops a process that separates the mixture | Attempts a partial separation process | Lists a technique without a process |
| **Scientific investigation** | Plans and conducts a controlled fair test; processes data and draws valid evidence-based conclusions | Plans a fair test and draws reasonable conclusions from data | Plans and conducts an investigation and draws a conclusion from data | Conducts an investigation with guidance | Records observations only |
| **Communicating** | Communicates ideas using accurate scientific vocabulary, diagrams and report structure | Communicates clearly with mostly accurate scientific language | Communicates ideas using scientific representations | Communicates with limited scientific language | Communicates with everyday language only |

### Note for unit alignment
The assessment is designed to gather evidence against the Year 7 Science achievement standard, with particular emphasis on: using the particle model to explain the physical properties of substances and developing processes that separate mixtures; and planning and conducting investigations and analysing data to identify patterns and draw conclusions.

# Year 7 Science — Culminating Assessment Task

## Mixtures & Separation: Designing a Water Purification Process

**Subject:** Science  |  **Year level:** Year 7  |  **Curriculum:** Australian Curriculum (v9)

### Task overview
A local creek has become contaminated with a mixture of sand, salt, vegetable oil and fine clay particles. Working as a junior process engineer, you must **design, justify and evaluate a multi-stage process to separate this mixture and produce the cleanest possible water sample.**

### What students produce
1. **Scientific report (800–1000 words)** explaining the particle-model reasoning behind each separation technique chosen (filtration, evaporation, decantation, sedimentation, use of magnets where relevant).
2. **Annotated process diagram** showing the order of separation stages and the property exploited at each stage (particle size, solubility, density, magnetism).
3. **Practical investigation log** documenting a fair test of ONE separation stage, including a hypothesis, controlled/independent/dependent variables, results table and a conclusion drawn from the data.

### Conditions
- Completed over 3 lessons (2 in-class practical + 1 report writing), plus homework.
- Individual report; investigation may be conducted in pairs.
- Students may use the class particle-model glossary and their lab notebooks.

### Purpose
This task is the culminating performance for the unit. It requires students to integrate their understanding of the particle model, the properties of pure substances and mixtures, and scientific investigation skills (planning a fair test, processing data, drawing evidence-based conclusions).
